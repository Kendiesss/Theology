The purpose of the document “Christus Vivit” is to demonstrate Pope Francis' wish for his audience to have a Christ-centered emphasis. He keeps extending invitations to the young people to meet and become friends with Jesus throughout the sermon. Knowing that Christus Vivit means "Christ is Alive.”

Persuading friends to visit churches and attend mass

I will use the skills I have developed as an altar server to do the following:

Persuading friends to visit churches and attend mass - attending Holy Mass will undoubtedly help them understand who God is. That, in my opinion, is the most original method to build a close relationship with God and the young people who do not know him.

Using my voice to introduce who is God and to the Catholic Faith

I will make it clear to him/her that Christ is alive and that he is always available to hear our prayers. Pope Francis encourages young people to fully live life to the fullest with Christ. And from what I have discovered it motivates them to develop their full potential for God's honor and the benefit of others. It is important to accomplish things with purpose and direction rather than just doing them. So that is why I cope with the idea of using my skills, knowledge, and my presence as an Altar Server.

Create opportunities for skill development

Find ways to make it possible for young people to grow and develop their talents. This can entail putting them in touch with organizations, training sessions, or workshops that are pertinent to their interests. Encourage children to take part in events that provide them a stage to demonstrate their skills and earn experience, such as contests, exhibits, or performances.
